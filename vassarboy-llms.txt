# Vassar Boy

> A novel by Drew Cushing. Literary fiction set at Vassar College, 1985-86.

Vassar Boy is a fictional account of life at Vassar College in the academic year of 1985-1986. Introduced by the author in the first person, the story is then told in the third person. In good New Narrative tradition, the novel features a character named Drew. All other characters are entirely fictional.

## About the Novel

- Genre: Literary Fiction
- Setting: Vassar College, Poughkeepsie, NY, 1985-86
- Length: 60,000 words
- Tradition: New Narrative
- Status: Forthcoming
- Publisher: Bent Boy Books

A rollicking romp through sophomore year that reimagines Mary McCarthy's iconic ensemble for a wilder, funnier, and decidedly queerer era. From the Aula to the Mug, from the Palladium to BoyBar — Vassar Boy revels in small scandals, casual affairs, and the drama of privileged youth unmoored from consequence.

## About the Author

Drew Cushing is the bastard son of New Narrative and Language Poetry. He holds an MFA in Creative Writing from San Francisco State University and an AB in Dramatic Literature from Vassar College. He is a graduate of Trinity Repertory Company and Conservatory, where he learned Viewpoints from Anne Bogart. His writing has appeared in The Fabulist, ZYZZYVA, Cathay, Laundry Pen, and other publications. He publishes under the imprint Bent Boy Books.

## Characters

- Ford: Tall, fabulously thin, Southern, rich, curly-haired, thrown out of several prep schools
- Mary Ann Kravitz (Makra): Connecticut girl, devoted to her art and her Stoli
- Jason: Drew's freshman roommate, committed preppy, plaids upon plaids
- Nancy Jane Pruit (Prissy): Upstate New York girl, excessively prudish
- Drew: Connecticut boy, Mayflower lineage, unstoppable libido, big American cars
- Todd: Sexy Chicago boy whose hair changes colors at whim
- Sally: South American heiress, cacao plantation heiress, Swiss banking mother
